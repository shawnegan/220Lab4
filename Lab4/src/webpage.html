Dogs

A dog can be a wonderful addition to any home, but whether you're an experienced pet parent or a first-time adopter, it's important to keep your canine companion's health and happiness a top priority. Below are some useful tips for all dog parents.

Feeding your Dog

Premium-quality dry food provides a well-balanced diet for adult dogs and may be mixed with water, broth or canned food. Your dog may enjoy cottage cheese, cooked egg or fruits and vegetables, but these additions should not total more than ten percent of his daily food intake.

Exercise

Exercise your dog regularly to keep your dog stimulated and healthy, and to keep their weight in check. Different breeds need different levels of exercise. Before adopting a dog, research the energy level of the breed to ensure you will be able to provide the proper level of exercise.

Puppy Care

Puppies are adorable, and quite tempting when one is looking to adopt. However, keep in mind that for the first year the amount of care a puppy requires is quite high, almost to the level of a toddler. If you think you will be unable to provide that level of care, please consider adopting an older dog.
